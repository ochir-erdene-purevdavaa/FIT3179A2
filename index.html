Australian Biodiversity

Overview of species richness by state:

This map highlights the total number of species recorded in each Australian state. Darker colors indicate higher biodiversity. Some regions host rare or endangered species that require conservation efforts.

Functional group richness by state:

This map shows how many species belong to each functional group (Reptiles, Birds, Mammals, Fish, Insects, Other) per state. Some species are still endangered, while others are abundant. Compare biodiversity patterns across groups and regions.

Species Richness by State:

This bar chart shows the total number of species in each state. Some states have very high species diversity, while others host fewer species. Conservation priorities vary accordingly.

Functional Group Richness by State:

This stacked bar chart breaks down the total species into functional groups (Reptiles, Birds, Mammals, Fish, Insects, Other) per state. It helps identify which groups dominate in different regions and highlights conservation needs.

Relative Abundance of Australia's Species:

This heat map visualizes the relative abundance of species across states and functional groups. Some areas show high biodiversity, while others are critical habitats for endangered species.
